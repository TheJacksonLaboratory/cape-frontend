Here we present an R package for the Combined Analysis of Epistasis and Pleiotropy, or cape. This package implements a method, originally described in Carter et al. (2012), that infers directed interaction networks between genetic variants for predicting the influence of genetic perturbations on phenotypes. This method takes advantage of complementary information in partially pleiotropic genetic variants to resolve directional influences between variants that interact epistatically. cape can be applied to a variety of genetic variants, such as single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs), copy number variations (CNVs) or structural variations (SVs). Here we demonstrate the functionality of cape by inferring a predictive network between quantitative trait loci (QTL) in a cross between the non-obese, non-diabetic (NON) mouse and the New Zealand obese (NZO) mouse (Reifsnyder (2000)).
